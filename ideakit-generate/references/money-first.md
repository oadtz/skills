# Money-first framing — "give me ideas where the money is"

Optional framing for when the user wants ideas sourced *from where the money concentrates*, rather than
from a fixed domain or their edge. It does not replace the normal workflow — it **re-points Step 2
(Scan) and re-weights Step 4 (Score)** toward money signals, and it is bound by one hard rule below.

## The hard rule (this is non-negotiable — "stand on principle, don't make it up")

**Every "the money is here" claim must rest on real, cited evidence — never on vibes or a remembered
fact.** "Where money is" is the single easiest place to hallucinate a lucrative-sounding market that
isn't. So:

- Each money claim cites a real signal *fetched this run*: funding rounds, budget/spend data, pricing
  pages, job postings (a role being hired = budget being spent), procurement/RFPs, or quantified
  complaints + willingness-to-pay.
- If you can't source it, label it a **hypothesis to validate**, not a fact — and route it to
  `ideakit-validate`. Do not present a guess as a finding (same discipline as `ideakit-validate`'s
  "do not invent market data").
- Prefer **revealed** money (what people/companies already pay) over **stated** interest (Mom Test).

## The principles this framing stands on (all real, named)

1. **Starving Crowd (Hormozi)** — the *market* beats the offer: a hungry crowd that is **able to pay,
   reachable, and growing** is where money is. Already the "Market" axis in `references/scoring.md`;
   money-first weights it hardest.
2. **Profit Pools (Gadiesh & Gilbert, HBR 1998)** — look past revenue/TAM to where **profit actually
   concentrates** along the value chain; a big-revenue space can be a *thin* profit pool, and a small
   one can be deep. Chase the deep pool, not the big number. ([HBR](https://hbr.org/1998/05/profit-pools-a-fresh-look-at-strategy))
3. **Value Migration (Slywotzky, 1995)** — value/profit *moves over time* from outmoded business
   designs to better ones. Ideate where value is **migrating to** (the leading edge), not where it
   sits today (already crowded). Funding flows and budget shifts are the leading indicators.
   ([overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Value_Migration_(book)))
4. **Painkiller > Vitamin + willingness-to-pay** — money concentrates where pain is *urgent* and buyers
   *already pay*; verify with a pre-sell, not a survey (behavior over words).
5. **Existing-budget principle** — selling into a budget line that *already exists* is where money is
   provably available; creating brand-new demand is harder and slower.

## The solo correction (do not skip — it's the whole trap)

For a **solo founder**, "where the money is" is **not** the biggest TAM, nor the most-funded/most-hyped
category. Those are usually crowded, slow, or winnable only with scale a solo doesn't have (the
classic "huge market, but I can't capture any of it"). For a solo, money is where there are **many
reachable, fast-paying buyers with high willingness-to-pay** — a deep, *accessible* slice of the profit
pool. Hold both Profit Pools (depth, not size) and the solo lens (reachable + fast, not just big).

## How it changes the workflow

- **Step 2 — Scan (lead with money evidence).** In addition to the normal sources, lead with:
  *funding flows* (YC RFS, a16z Big Ideas, what's getting funded in RBF/VC), *budget/spend signals*
  (job postings, software spend, procurement/RFPs), *pricing pages* (what buyers already pay), and
  *complaint + WTP* signals. Map the **profit pool** (where margin is deep) and **value migration**
  (where it's moving). Cite every signal; record "couldn't source it → hypothesis."
- **Step 4 — Score (re-weight).** Weight **Market / Starving Crowd** and **Survivability** hardest, and
  add an explicit check: *is this money reachable by a solo, and does it pay fast?* Apply the existing
  fad/Lindy flag — hyped money that's a fad (Candy) is a trap, not a pool.
- **Step 5 — Synthesize (declare the money axis).** Alongside value-type and durability, declare each
  shortlisted idea's **profit-pool depth** and **solo-reachability**, and explicitly flag any
  "big-TAM-but-thin-or-unreachable" idea as the trap to avoid.

## Hand-off

Money-first sourcing still only *recommends*; the money claims are hypotheses until proven. Send the
winner to `ideakit-validate` (confirm market size / WTP / survivability with evidence) and, for the
revenue model itself, to `solo-model`. Never let "this looks lucrative" skip validation.
