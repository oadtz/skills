# Breakthrough mode

This is the default generation procedure. Skip it only when the main skill selects explicit standard
mode. It is a higher-search-cost procedure for ideas that should feel surprising, defensible, and
enterable—not a celebrity persona and not a license for random weirdness.

## 1. Freeze a neutral invention packet

Give every invention lane the same compact packet:

- the actor/domain or force brief and the desired decision;
- Observed signals, causal openings, counter-signals, and uncertainty;
- founder-specific access, credibility, lived evidence, and no-go work.

Withhold candidate products, the previous portfolio, obvious categories, and other lanes' output.
Record the founder's time, cash, support capacity, and target-to-revenue separately for the later solo
translation. Do not let feasibility filters collapse divergent search before a mechanism exists.

## 2. Run isolated invention lanes

Use fresh contexts or subagents when available. A lane sees only the neutral packet and its operator.
It returns terse, anonymous seeds—not polished pitches. No lane is entitled to a finalist.

### Lane A — bedrock, deletion, and the new bottleneck

Decompose the outcome into physical or economic inputs. Ask what remains expensive when the headline
capability becomes 10× cheaper, which inherited step or assumption can disappear, and what scarce
complement becomes the new place where value pools.

### Lane B — expired premise and system response

Find a premise that customers, institutions, or incumbents still optimize around although the world
has changed. Trace the second- and third-order behavior, shifted power or budget, and the rational
incentive that keeps incumbents from serving the opening.

### Lane C — structured distant analogy

Represent the opening abstractly as actors, relationships, constraint, trigger, and feedback loop.
Find a remote domain with the same structure, then transfer its causal mechanism, ownership rule,
coordination pattern, or value capture. Reject surface metaphors and renamed category copies.

### Lane D — non-consumption, desire, and value capture

Start with actors excluded by expertise, cost, trust, geography, or workflow; or with identity,
status, ritual, belonging, and aspiration that a pain-only scan misses. Invert who uses, pays, owns,
approves, or benefits. Make the resulting transaction legible.

## 3. Generate, differentiate, then expand

Within each lane:

1. Write several short seeds as `actor | trigger/job | causal thesis | scarce asset | mechanism |
   proof/feedback loop | payer | architecture`.
2. Compare the seeds structurally. Replace any pair that shares actor + job + thesis + mechanism; a
   different feature, channel, or name is not enough.
3. Expand only the strongest one or two into a falsifiable thesis, wedge, value capture, and reason it
   may now work.

If fresh contexts are unavailable, run the lanes as separate scratch passes. Do not show a later lane
the prior lane's candidates or revise earlier seeds until synthesis.

## 4. Synthesize without averaging

Collect all lane outputs only after divergence ends. Canonicalize them, cluster duplicates, and retain
the strongest representative. Recombine two candidates only when their mechanisms reinforce each
other; do not create a feature bundle or compromise concept. The useful result may come from one lane,
several lanes, or a cross-lane recombination.

Assign a **venture-family ID** before scoring. If one company would serve the candidates as modules or
buying occasions using the same buyer relationship, input corpus, operating workflow, and compounding
asset, they are one venture family even when their trigger or pitch differs. Merge them and keep the
strongest wedge; a portfolio may contain only one finalist from a family. Use a four-field comparison:
matching on any three fields means one family. Do not override the result with a new name, trigger, or
asserted thesis.

## 5. Run the evaluator-only collision pass

Use a fresh evaluator context when possible. Only now generate the obvious baseline a competent generic
generator would propose. The inventor must never see this baseline beforehand.

For each candidate, compare the canonical signature against:

- the obvious baseline;
- current offerings and failed or adjacent prior art, searched by mechanism as well as category;
- the user's previous, saved, and killed ideas when available;
- other candidates in this run.

Kill or merge a structural collision. Compare the scarce asset and proof or feedback loop, not only the
full signature: a different actor, payer, vertical, trigger, or channel is insufficient when the causal
thesis and core mechanism are the same. Ownership or architecture counts only when it changes how value
is created or captured. State the material difference for anything retained. A search can reveal a
collision; it cannot prove that an idea is globally novel.

## 6. Translate to a solo-founder entry

Now reintroduce the operating envelope. For each survivor, specify:

- the smallest paid proof and the exact first buying occasion;
- how the founder can reach the first 10 without a hypothetical audience;
- cash, hours, support, liability, trust, and dependency burden;
- every independent commitment required before value can be demonstrated; pre-commit multi-party
  dependencies—including non-paying parties whose data or behavior is essential—before spending;
- capacity economics: test price, sustainable caseload, contribution after support, and a credible
  route away from founder hours if the desired game requires it;
- a manual, service-first, channel, licensing, or narrower-wedge translation if the full architecture
  is not yet enterable;
- the affordable-loss test for the killer risk.

Capacity math must leave explicit room for selling, administration, learning, and delivery variance;
all available founder hours are not billable capacity. Separate the demand checkpoint from the value
checkpoint. `Paid commitment` is required before work. `Delivered value` is a separate observable buyer
decision or outcome after delivery; both must pass.

Do not shrink the long-term thesis merely to make it buildable. Separate the large architecture from
the founder-scale entry. Park a concept when no credible translation exists.

## 7. Apply the pursuable-wow finish line

Judge these separately; do not total them:

- **Surprise** — it escapes the obvious category through thesis or mechanism;
- **Inevitable in hindsight** — its causal chain makes the reveal click;
- **Enterable** — the founder can earn the right to learn with a bounded first move;
- **Value capture** — a named payer, buying occasion, and exchange support viable capacity economics
  or a credible leverage path.

A finalist cannot compensate for failing one with strength on another. It also needs an honest killer
risk and a falsifiable test of payment plus delivered value—not merely participation. Express its core
as `Revelation`, `Why it feels inevitable`, `Why others miss it`, `Solo entry`, `Value capture`,
`Paid commitment`, `Delivered value`, and `Killer risk` without narrating the hidden process. Return
fewer finalists rather than admitting a near-duplicate or one with broken solo economics.

## Anti-patterns

- impersonating a famous founder or treating biography as a reasoning method;
- exposing the obvious baseline before invention and anchoring every lane to it;
- letting lanes see and converge on one another before synthesis;
- using founder time or budget to prune divergence instead of translating the survivor;
- rewarding a lane quota, rhetorical shock, exotic technology, or clever naming;
- using “first”, “only”, “nobody”, or another unverified global novelty claim to manufacture surprise;
- presenting “Why others miss it” as universal observed behavior rather than a labeled inference;
- preserving one venture family as several finalists because it has several buying occasions;
- treating a low pilot price as enterability when sustainable capacity economics do not work;
- calling a familiar category novel because its feature list or target vertical changed.
