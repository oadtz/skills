# Coverage self-audit — run inside Step 2, every run

This is the governing principle (*reflexivity*, plus *requisite variety* of inputs) applied to
**scanning**: make your frame explicit and deliberately gather signal from outside it.

## Why this exists

The "desire vs. pain" blind spot (which made this skill miss the BL/Y-series boom) was just *one*
named instance of a deeper failure: **a generator only sees what its sources and rubric point at,
and every source list has a horizon.** Patching blind spots one at a time ("now add fandom", "now
add geography") is a losing chase. The general fix is a repeatable step that makes the skill *audit
its own coverage and deliberately scan against its own bias* before generating — so it surfaces the
*unknown* unknowns, not just the ones we've already named.

This is the difference between widening one door and installing a window that finds new doors.

## How to run it (2 minutes, before Step 3)

After your first pass of scanning, **stop and ask: "Given the sources and lens I just used, what
kinds of markets am I structurally unable to see right now?"** Walk the axes of blindness below.
For each axis, locate where your current scan sits, name the opposite end, and **deliberately run at
least one extra search at the 1–2 opposite ends most relevant to the brief.** Then proceed to
generate.

## Axes of blindness (the checklist)

Run each as "where am I sitting → what's the opposite I haven't scanned?"

- **Value type** — painkiller/utility ↔ desire/identity (entertainment, fandom, status, play, IP).
- **Geography / culture** — Western/English sources (YC, a16z, HN, G2) ↔ local & non-Western signal
  (Thai/SEA, Chinese/Korean/Japanese platforms, informal economy, regional regulators).
- **Who pays** — clear B2B budget ↔ attention/ad-funded, two-sided, prosumer, government/grant,
  donation/community-funded, creator-monetized.
- **Buyer sophistication** — tech/early-adopter ↔ mainstream, low-tech, offline, or underserved
  populations who never show up in review sites or GitHub.
- **Trigger of the trend** — tech capability (new model/API) ↔ regulation, demographic shift,
  cultural/values movement, supply-chain or geopolitical change, climate.
- **Maturity / where value is moving** — commoditizing components ↔ newly-possible ones (see Wardley
  lens below). Opportunity clusters at the boundary where something just became cheap/standard.
- **Time horizon** — works today ↔ bets on a capability arriving in 12–18 months.

You will not scan every opposite every time. Pick the axes where your scan is most lopsided for
*this* brief, and cover at least two opposite-ends you'd otherwise have skipped.

## Optional lens: Wardley mapping (for "what others can't see yet")

When the goal is to spot opportunities competitors miss, map the value chain of the user's domain
and place each component on its evolution stage: **genesis → custom-built → product → commodity.**
Opportunities concentrate where a component is *industrializing* (becoming a cheap commodity/API) —
because that collapses cost and unlocks new things to build *on top*. Ask: "What just became cheap
or standard here, and what does that newly make possible one layer up?" This catches structural
trends that pure complaint-mining and trend-list-reading both miss.

## Output of the audit

A one-line note in your working pass: *"Default scan was skewed toward [X]; I additionally scanned
[opposite-1] and [opposite-2], which surfaced [signal/none]."* Recording "none" is fine and useful —
it shows the axis was checked, not assumed. The deliberate counter-bias searches then feed Step 3
exactly like the primary scan does.

## Anti-patterns

- Treating this audit as "also add fandom" — it's a *general* coverage check, not a second fixed
  source list. The axes are prompts to find what you're missing *this run*, whatever it is.
- Running the audit but generating only from the comfortable (default) signal anyway. The point is
  to let the counter-bias scans actually change the idea set.
- Auditing sources but not the *rubric*: a painkiller-only rubric will still suppress desire ideas
  even if you scanned for them. Coverage spans both what you look at and how you score it.
