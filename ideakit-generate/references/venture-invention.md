# Venture invention lenses

Use this reference after signals have been synthesized into opportunity theses. Select the few lenses
that create productive tension for the thesis; never march through all of them.

## Opportunity recognition

- **Entrepreneurial alertness** — notice underpriced resources, information asymmetry, value migration,
  non-consumption, and work that incumbents ignore because it is initially too small or awkward.
- **Abductive reasoning** — ask what plausible new explanation connects several observations. Record
  the observation, inference bridge, resulting bet, and what would falsify it.
- **Systems / second-order effects** — trace change → behavior → bottleneck → shifted power or spend.
  The new bottleneck after something becomes cheap or abundant is often the opportunity.

## Invention moves

- **Invert** who performs, pays, owns, approves, or benefits.
- **Remove** the most expensive step, handoff, actor, or assumption.
- **Recombine** mechanisms from distant categories around the same job.
- **Unbundle / rebundle** a bloated suite or a fragmented user-assembled workflow.
- **Serve non-consumption**: make the outcome reachable by people excluded by expertise, cost, trust,
  geography, or workflow complexity.
- **Productize labor** or begin service-first to discover what should become product.
- **Exploit the new bottleneck** created when a capability becomes abundant.
- **Change the game** with Blue Ocean ERRC: eliminate, reduce, raise, create—but only where the change
  alters buyer value or economics.
- **Design from identity/desire** when belonging, taste, status, ritual, or self-expression drives the
  job more strongly than pain.

## Entrepreneur pass

Use Effectuation under uncertainty:

- start from the founder's means (skills, access, credibility, relationships);
- cap downside with affordable loss;
- recruit the first committed stakeholders rather than forecasting an abstract market;
- treat surprises as information and possible leverage.

For each concept, make the business legible:

1. Trigger and first user: who looks for this, in what moment?
2. Buyer and budget: who pays, from what existing spend or newly created value?
3. Wedge: what narrow outcome can be won manually or with a thin product?
4. Distribution: how do the first 10 users become reachable without “do marketing”?
5. Expansion: what adjacent job, actor, or transaction opens next?
6. Compounding power: which plausible Seven Power could form—network, scale, switching cost, brand,
   cornered resource, process power, or counter-positioning? Do not manufacture a moat on day one.
7. Affordable-loss test: what is the cheapest action that resolves the most dangerous uncertainty?

## Portfolio logic

Do not collapse judgment into one total score. Select a small portfolio by role when each role has a
defensible candidate:

- **Asymmetric bet** — cheap to learn, large upside.
- **Contrarian bet** — depends on a clear belief the market may not share yet.
- **Fast-cash wedge** — shortest credible path to a paying user.
- **Compounding venture** — strongest path from wedge to durable power.
- **Wildcard** — most unfamiliar mechanism that still survives the reasoning and entrepreneur passes.

Leave a role empty rather than fill it with a weak concept. Explain editorial judgment when a riskier
concept advances over a safer one.
