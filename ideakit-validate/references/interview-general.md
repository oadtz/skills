# Interview Question Bank — General Project Mode

Use this question bank during Phase 1 when the seed is a non-software initiative — a marketing
campaign, content series, business initiative, operational change, research project, event, etc.

Walk through themes in order. Adapt — drill down on vague answers, skip questions already answered,
surface contradictions when you hear them. Aim for 6–12 total questions.

## Theme 1 — The Outcome (start here)

- "What's the outcome you're going after? When this is done, what's different in the world or in
  your business?"
- "Why does this matter right now? What's the trigger or opportunity?"
- "What happens if you don't do it?"

**Drill-down triggers:**
- Vague outcome ("more growth") → "What number would tell you this worked? Over what timeframe?"

## Theme 2 — The Audience or Beneficiary

- "Who is this for? Who needs to notice, change behavior, or benefit?"
- "How does this audience think about the topic today? What do they currently believe or do?"
- "How will you reach them?"

## Theme 3 — The Approach

- "What's the rough shape of how you'd execute this? (Campaign, event, content series, process
  change, partnership, etc.)"
- "Have you or anyone you know done something similar? What worked, what didn't?"
- "Is there an obvious 'standard' way to do this — and are you doing the standard way or
  something different? Why?"

## Theme 4 — Constraints & Resources

- "What's your timeline? Hard deadline or flexible?"
- "What's your budget — money, time, headcount?"
- "Who needs to be involved? Whose buy-in do you need?"
- "Any constraints that aren't obvious — brand guidelines, regulatory issues, internal politics,
  existing commitments?"

## Theme 5 — Definition of Done

- "What's the smallest meaningful version of this? (E.g., one pilot event vs. a series; one
  flagship piece vs. a full editorial calendar.)"
- "What's explicitly NOT part of this initiative?"
- "How will you know it worked? What metrics or signals?"

## Theme 6 — Risks & Assumptions (use sparingly)

- "What has to go right for this to work?"
- "What's the part you're least sure about?"
- "If a peer pushed back on this, what's the strongest objection they'd raise?"

## Closing the Interview

When you have enough material, write the **idea snapshot** (see SKILL.md Phase 1 for format) and
ask the user to confirm or correct before moving to research.

If the user said "I don't know" to anything important, list those as **open questions to research**
and explicitly call them out — don't quietly drop them.
