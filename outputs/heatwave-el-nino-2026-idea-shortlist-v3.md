## Frame

Brief type: **force/event + money-first**.

Force: global heatwaves in 2026, worsening extreme heat, and a strengthening El Nino. I treat "super
El Nino" carefully: NOAA CPC gives a 63% chance of a **very strong** El Nino during Nov 2026-Jan 2027,
while WMO says El Nino has developed and is forecast to strengthen rapidly into a strong event.

Goal: find ideas that can plausibly grow into a million-scale main project through organic growth,
venture/funding, grants/public funding, or a hybrid path.

## Raw idea pool

1. **Heatwave Index** — heat-livability ratings for cities, neighborhoods, rentals, schools, offices
   and travel destinations.
2. **Night Shift City** — local guide and marketplace for heat-safe evening/night activities, cool
   venues, late retail, shaded routes and events.
3. **40C Life Kit** — DTC heat-adapted daily-life kits for commute, sleep, hydration, UV and cooling
   textiles.
4. **WorkRest Shield** — heat-safe scheduling, WBGT alerts, check-ins, compliance logs and
   insurer-ready risk data for outdoor/non-cooled work.
5. **HeatGrant OS** — grant and project operating system for heat resilience programs, from proposal
   to outcomes reporting.
6. **Water Habit Loop** — household/community water-saving challenge using smart-meter feedback,
   rebates, device bundles and neighborhood goals.
7. **El Nino Pantry Radar** — consumer + food-buyer intelligence for crop risk, grocery swaps,
   no-cook menus and sourcing substitutions.
8. **Heatwear Lab** — heat-safe apparel/textile curation and product testing for workers, commuters
   and creators.
9. **ShadeShare** — rentals and micro-franchise marketplace for tents, shade, misting, cooling rooms
   and event/school kits.
10. **Heat Buddy Network** — community check-in network for elderly people, disabled people, children,
    outdoor workers and pets.
11. **Climate Comfort Score for Rentals** — renter-facing score/checklist for apartment heat risk,
    cooling cost, night temperature, shade and ventilation.
12. **CoolCart** — weather-aware retail and F&B demand engine for inventory, promos and opening-hour
    tactics.
13. **Street Vendor Heat Kit** — shade, hydration, schedule and micro-insurance bundle for informal
    vendors.
14. **School Heat Day Kit** — school playbooks, parent comms, safe-room supplies and grant templates.
15. **Heat Liability File** — audit trail for landlords, schools, employers and event operators.
16. **Cold Meal Club** — creator/community product for no-cook meals, hydration recipes and
    heatwave grocery baskets.

## Sub-scores

### #1 Heatwave Index — 26/30

- Pull 5/5 — heat affects where people live, travel, study, work and sleep.
- Market 4/5 — broad consumer audience; monetization can stack through reports, affiliates, real estate,
  travel, schools and public-sector data.
- Secret 5/5 — heat is still treated as weather, but it is becoming a place-quality score like
  walkability or air quality.
- Job 5/5 — decide where to rent, buy, travel, send kids, work and retrofit.
- Survivability 4/5 — brand + scoring methodology + user reviews + local dataset + marketplace links.
- Feasibility 3/5 — easy content MVP; harder to make scoring trusted.
- Cheapest test — publish 20 city/neighborhood heat-livability rankings and test email signups, paid
  local reports, and renter checklist downloads.

### #2 Night Shift City — 24/30

- Pull 4/5 — people want to keep living, eating and socializing without daytime heat.
- Market 3/5 — local monetization is fragmented but real through venues, events, tourism and ads.
- Secret 5/5 — heat creates a new urban time layer: the city moves after sunset.
- Job 4/5 — find what is open, cool, safe and worth doing after heat peaks.
- Survivability 4/5 — local creator network + venue relationships + city-specific community.
- Feasibility 4/5 — can start as Instagram/TikTok/site in one hot city.
- Cheapest test — run a 30-day "after 6pm cool city" guide in Bangkok, Phoenix, Madrid or Sydney and
  sell 5 venue placements.

### #3 WorkRest Shield — 25/30

- Pull 5/5 — heat creates safety, productivity, liability and delay costs.
- Market 5/5 — construction, logistics, farms, manufacturing, insurers and brokers have budgets.
- Secret 4/5 — the wedge is not another alert; it is operationalizing heat into schedules, logs and
  financial risk evidence.
- Job 4/5 — keep work going safely while creating compliance/insurance proof.
- Survivability 4/5 — exposure data + insurance/broker distribution + workflow switching costs.
- Feasibility 3/5 — MVP is simple; distribution is the hard part.
- Cheapest test — 30-day SMS/check-in pilot with one contractor, farm or event operator.

### #4 HeatGrant OS — 24/30

- Pull 4/5 — heat resilience is urgent; funding exists, but procurement is slow.
- Market 5/5 — grants and resilience programs are visible; small cities/NGOs need capacity.
- Secret 4/5 — the opportunity is grant ops + measurable outcomes, not another heat map.
- Job 5/5 — identify, fund, run and report heat interventions.
- Survivability 4/5 — grant templates + outcomes data + implementation-partner network.
- Feasibility 2/5 — service-first MVP is possible; scalable SaaS takes time.
- Cheapest test — offer a "heat grant readiness sprint" to 5 local governments or community orgs.

### #5 Water Habit Loop — 23/30

- Pull 4/5 — water stress becomes emotional when restrictions, bills and shortages appear.
- Market 4/5 — utilities, device sellers and households all benefit from measurable reduction.
- Secret 4/5 — water conservation can be designed like behavior change and community pride, not only
  utility scolding.
- Job 4/5 — know water use, cut waste, find rebates/devices and compare progress locally.
- Survivability 3/5 — local participation data and utility/device partnerships.
- Feasibility 4/5 — can test manually with 100 households and affiliate/device partners.
- Cheapest test — run a neighborhood water-saving challenge with before/after bills and one property
  manager, HOA or utility.

### #6 El Nino Pantry Radar — 22/30

- Pull 4/5 — food price and availability shocks hit both households and food businesses.
- Market 4/5 — food buyers pay for sourcing certainty; consumers and creators engage with practical
  grocery/menu swaps.
- Secret 3/5 — forecasts matter only when translated into substitutions and timing decisions.
- Job 4/5 — decide what to buy, substitute, stock, menu or source before prices move.
- Survivability 3/5 — needs localized playbooks, supplier links or trusted creator distribution; pure
  information is copyable.
- Feasibility 4/5 — newsletter/report/community MVP is cheap.
- Cheapest test — sell a USD99 "El Nino grocery/menu watch" to 50 restaurants, grocers or serious home
  cooks; pair with free creator content for distribution.

## Killed / deprioritized

- Generic heat-alert app — weather apps and governments can absorb it.
- Pure cooling-center locator — socially useful but likely a public-good feature; better inside
  HeatGrant OS or Heat Buddy Network.
- Hydration tracker — weak standalone willingness-to-pay; better as part of 40C Life Kit or Cold Meal
  Club.
- Heatwear-only label — promising but crowded; stronger as 40C Life Kit or tested product curation.
- ShadeShare — promising service-first marketplace, but operationally heavy; good later after a city
  wedge.
- Climate dashboard — too generic and B2B-samey.

## Recommendation

If I had to pick one v3 winner, I would still pick **Heatwave Index**.

Why it wins: it is not B2B-first, not grant-dependent, and not a commodity cooling product. It starts
as a consumer/media/data product, can grow organically through rankings and local guides, and later
compounds into reports, real-estate/travel/school data, retrofit marketplace, and public-sector tools.

If the priority is faster institutional revenue, choose **WorkRest Shield**. If the priority is grant
logic, choose **HeatGrant OS**. If the priority is culturally interesting organic growth, test **Night
Shift City**.
