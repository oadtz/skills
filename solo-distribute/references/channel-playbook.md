# Channel playbook for solo founders

The principle: *distribution is the product's twin; one channel deep beats five shallow.* Pick the
single channel that sits where the buyer already gathers **and** fits the founder's strengths. Each
channel below notes what it suits, the founder strength it rewards, rough time-to-traction, and the
solo watch-out.

## The channels

### Build-in-public (audience-led)
Share the journey, numbers, and progress on X/LinkedIn/a blog; let an audience accumulate around you.
- **Suits:** products whose buyers are themselves online founders/makers/creators; founders willing to
  write/share consistently. **Rewards:** writing + candor. **Traction:** months (compounding).
- **Watch-out:** an audience of *peers* isn't always an audience of *buyers* — make sure the people you
  attract actually have the problem and the budget.

### SEO / content
Rank for the queries the buyer types; let search deliver buyers on autopilot over time.
- **Suits:** problems people actively search for; B2B and tools. **Rewards:** writing + patience +
  some craft. **Traction:** slow (3–9+ months) but durable and low-touch once it compounds.
- **Watch-out:** slow to start; quality bar rising. Best paired with an email capture so traffic
  becomes owned audience.

### Community presence
Show up where buyers already ask questions (subreddits, Slack/Discord, forums, niche groups) and be
genuinely helpful; sell softly through reputation.
- **Suits:** narrow niches with an active gathering place. **Rewards:** helpfulness + consistency.
  **Traction:** weeks–months. **Watch-out:** self-promotion rules — earn standing before pitching.

### Direct outreach / founder-led sales
Personally contact people with the problem (email, DM, calls). The classic "do things that don't
scale" engine for the first customers.
- **Suits:** B2B, higher-priced offers, productized services. **Rewards:** talking + persistence.
  **Traction:** fast (days–weeks) and the best *teacher* of your real message. **Watch-out:** doesn't
  scale on one person's time — use it to *learn* and land the first 10–100, then systematize what worked.

### Marketplaces & launches
Distribute through an existing marketplace (app/plugin store, template marketplace) or a launch event
(Product-Hunt-style, community launch).
- **Suits:** Stairstep step-1 products; tools that live in a platform's ecosystem. **Rewards:**
  packaging + timing. **Traction:** a spike, not a steady stream. **Watch-out:** a launch is an event,
  not a strategy — capture emails so the spike converts to owned audience.

### Paid acquisition
Ads (search/social) to a landing page.
- **Suits:** proven offers with known conversion and margins that support CAC. **Rewards:** analytical
  iteration + budget. **Traction:** fast but costs money and punishes an unvalidated offer.
- **Watch-out:** usually *premature* for a solo founder pre-product-market-fit — you'll burn cash
  learning what free channels teach for free. Revisit once conversion is proven.

### Partnerships / integrations
Get in front of another product's audience via integrations, co-marketing, affiliates.
- **Suits:** tools that complement a bigger product; later-stage. **Rewards:** relationship-building.
  **Traction:** slow to set up, strong once live. **Watch-out:** dependency on the partner.

## Picking one (founder-strength × buyer-location)

| If the founder is strongest at… | and buyers gather at… | start with |
|---|---|---|
| writing, candor | X/LinkedIn, maker communities | build-in-public + email list |
| writing, patience | Google / search | SEO/content + email capture |
| helping, conversation | a specific subreddit/Slack/forum | community presence |
| talking, persistence | reachable B2B inboxes | direct outreach / founder sales |
| packaging, timing | a platform's ecosystem | marketplace + a focused launch |

Commit to one. Name the second for later. Resist running several at once — that's the exact failure
the principle guards against.

## The "first 100 customers" play (manual, before systematizing)

1. **List 50–100 specific people/orgs** with the problem (not a segment — names).
2. **Reach them personally** where they already are; lead with help, not a pitch.
3. **Hand-hold the first users** — onboard them yourself; harvest the words they use (that's your
   future copy) and the objections (that's your roadmap).
4. **Pre-sell where possible** — money now + validation (loop back to `solo-fund`).
5. **Only then systematize** the one motion that produced customers into a repeatable weekly cadence.
   Set the cadence small enough to sustain for a year — consistency, handed to `solo-sustain`, is what
   makes any of this compound.
