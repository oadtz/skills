# Investor one-pager — managed clinic night shift

Audience: seed investor. Decision: fund a three-clinic paid pilot. Belief shift: missed bookings come
from broken escalation ownership, not merely slow replies. Ask: release $25,000 against three named
milestones.

Fact: two managers shared handoff logs ([evidence](https://example.com/logs)). Inference: a managed
shift may outperform another inbox tool. Ambition: become the operating layer for after-hours patient
coordination.

Observation: escalation ownership changes at closing time. Consequence: bookings and urgent cases can
fall between shifts. Response and mechanism: a human-led night desk follows clinic-authored rules and
returns an auditable handoff.

Counter-case and uncertainty: labor intensity may prevent acceptable margins. The pilot measures
escalations per shift, manager overrides, retained bookings, and willingness to renew.
