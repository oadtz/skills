# Synthetic breakthrough contract fixture — not market evidence

## Continuity escrow

**Observed:** A placeholder operator repeats a brittle handoff after each software change. [Synthetic source](https://example.com/continuity)

**Inferred:** When code production gets cheaper, continuity and accountable recovery can become the scarce complement.

**Bet:** A narrow buyer will pay to preserve operating intent across disposable implementations. Wrong if ordinary documentation and warranties already solve the handoff.

`Revelation:` The durable product is not the application; it is an executable record of what the business refuses to let a replacement application forget.

Why others miss it: Most searches follow falling build cost toward more software, instead of asking which obligation survives every rebuild.

Solo entry: Sell a paid manual continuity review to one agency's clients, capped at a founder-owned pilot and an affordable-loss budget before writing software.

Value capture: The SME pays a fixed review fee at vendor handoff; a reusable protocol increases contribution per case without requiring always-on support.

Paid commitment: The SME prepays the fixed review fee before the continuity review begins.

Delivered value: The buyer uses the completed pack to approve a vendor handoff after one independent recovery rehearsal succeeds.

Killer risk: Buyers may treat continuity as the outgoing developer's responsibility; disconfirm the bet if no owner prepays for the first review.

The wedge is one high-cost recurring handoff. First-10 distribution runs through a named agency-owner community, not generic ads. The counter-case is that source-control history is sufficient. The cheapest test is a manual recovery rehearsal; if it works, verified operating histories may compound into switching power.
