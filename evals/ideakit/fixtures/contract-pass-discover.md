# Edge map — Mina

This is a provisional map, not an identity verdict.

## Behavioral trail and energy

Mina actually built three volunteer repair guides and spent weekends teaching neighbors. That lights
her up; repetitive client reporting drains her.

## Desired game and risk & resources

Desired game: a calm, useful solo business. Affordable loss: four weekends and $400.

## Reaction evidence

She preferred a field tool over a creator business, recoiled from agency work, and chose “combine
these.” “None of these / what is missing” remained available.

## Contradictions

She is skilled at consulting but does not want delivery calls at the center of the business.

## Candidate playing fields

1. Repair knowledge for renters — wrong if she dislikes maintaining technical material.
2. Tools for volunteer fixers — wrong if the audience will not pay or introduce an institutional buyer.

Riskiest hypotheses: whether teaching or tool-making is the enduring source of energy.
